FTL Framework: 15-Week Journey From the A Life to the F Life

Quick Rundown: A series of weekly articles with the goal of publishing a simple framework anyone can use to design and create an unconventional life.

Andrew Folts

Apr 10, 2019 at 2:08pm

Hey, my name's Andrew. This is a story about my life—but it's a story about your life, too. Five years ago, I graduated from a liberal arts college with a fancy degree and $50,000 in student loan debt...sound familiar?

If you're like me, you probably landed a full-time job where you could work the "right" hours, earn the "right" amount of money, and gain the "right" experience. Maybe you even made the "right" move by quitting to start your own business.

But after living the "A Life" for years...

Are you as creative as you'd like to be?

As productive?

As motivated?

Are you even happy?

Are you even free?

Or are you still stuck—like a tiny cog in a huge machine?

You can be more than a cog.

While escaping the 9–5 world can be a great first step toward actual freedom, a lot of freelancers find themselves even more trapped and even less happy than when they were working a corporate gig. How is that possible?

I'll tell you. It's all about the "lines."

Lines are rules. They're the social guard rails, the things you're "supposed" to do—the tracks you're expected to follow because that's "just the way things are." These lines are meant to make us feel safe and normal: if you just keep your head down and work hard enough, everything will be alright.

We both know that's bullshit.

Studies consistently show that most Americans are living paycheck–to–paycheck: in 2016, nearly half the people surveyed by the Federal Reserve Board said they would have a hard time coming up with $400 to pay for an emergency. $400!!

If you and I continue going down the same beaten path, we're going to spend the rest of our lives trading time for money, until we have been ground into the earth by the weight of other people's success—like that guy in the sandwich line wearing a back brace and wrist guard just to make it through the day.

It doesn't matter whether you're a CEO or a sandwich-maker. If you keep following the "lines," if you keep chasing the "A Life," you will always be just making it through the day. Because, frankly, the working world is DESIGNED to squeeze every last ounce of energy and productivity out of you—while someone else reaps the profit.

Fuck that. Fuck the lines.

What is F the Lines, really?

Finance buffs will teach you how to make money. Creative experts will help you improve your technical skills. Business gurus will advise you on starting a business. But most of the resources available to you are focused on making you better at FOLLOWING the rules.

Where can you go to learn how to break them? Who can help you combine all that raw experience and knowledge into a kind of life that doesn't exist yet?

I don't have all the answers.

But I created F the Lines to find them.

Let's go on an expedition.

Over the next four months (April 1st to August 1st), I'm going to be posting weekly articles on creativity, productivity, and happiness—with the end-goal of publishing a simple framework that anyone can use to create a nonconventional business and lifestyle. For anyone tuning in later, that's OK. I'll send you the backlog.

If you want to come along with me, you can subscribe below to get weekly articles with 100% actionable insights and tips. Sound helpful? Keep reading, I'm going to cover the topics I have planned and share a few articles to get you started.

The FTL Roadmap

When I said I don't have the answers, I meant it. What I do have is questions. I'm going to explore one of those questions each week and share the results until I accomplish my personal goals: buying and converting a camper van, starting a podcast, and switching to life on the road (more on this in my blueprint article next week).

Weekly Topics

  • How do I create a life blueprint?

  • How can I save up money and energy?

  • How do I create a productivity system?

  • What kind of skills and experience do I need?

  • How do I launch a lifestyle business?

  • How do I keep myself accountable?

  • How can I market your business?

  • When will I be considered an expert?

  • How can I share my knowledge with others?

  • When will I know if I'm actually free?
