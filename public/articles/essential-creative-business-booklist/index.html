The Win Without Pitching Manifesto

If you read only one book in your career, this should be it. The Win Without Pitching Manifesto can be powered through in a single evening, but the insights Blair Enns delivers on value, positioning, and client relationships will last a lifetime—and save you a world of frustration.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2R2v3he

Favorite Quote: "When we win by charging less, price becomes or positioning...we become the price shop." —Blaire Enns, p. 115

7 Strategies for Wealth & Happiness

Cheesy as it may be, Jim Rohn's advice in 7SFWAH is both solid and timeless. In each section, he lays out a simple strategy for effective goal-setting, learning, organization, and motivation. You'll find his happy-go-lucky mindset both annoying and highly persuasive.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2rHBAiW

Favorite Quote: "If you don't have a list of goals, I can guess your bank balance within a few hundred dollars." —Earl Shoaff, p.21

DotCom Secrets: The Underground Playbook for Growing Your Company Online

Whether you think Russel Brunson is a genius or a scam artist, DCS is a must-read for anyone selling products or services online. The book covers how to set up effective sales funnels, build "value ladders," and learn from your competitor's. The goofy hand-drawn examples and simplified terminology make it surprisingly easy to pick up a laundry list of concepts—even if the word "sales" normally makes you groan.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2D7u6fr

Favorite Quote: "Imagine that your clients could pay you anything to get a desired result. What, then, would you do to help guarantee their success? Where would you lead them? What does that place look like?" —Russel Brunson, p. 19

In the Company of Women

Grace Bonney offers a simple, but potent glimpse into the lives and workspaces of over a hundred different women—many of whom may serve as a reminder of who you are as a creator and why you started your business in the first place.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2UVeGSz

Favorite Quote: "Let go or be dragged." —Michele Quan, p. 24

NLP: The Essential Guide to Neuro-Linguistic Programming

How do you become a better person? Tough question. There’s no magic potion, but if you’re looking to improve your confidence and social skills, NLP is an easy way to start making progress quickly. Co-author Tom Hoobyar lays out a bunch of simple strategies for changing your own thought processes—so you can build rapport with people, fix negative habits, and take control of your life.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2YmAh8u

Favorite Quote: “The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place.” —George Bernard Shaw, p. 202

The Designer's Dictionary of Color

Pay no mind to the word "Designer" is this book's title. TDDC is a very accessible and handy reference for any entrepreneur. The descriptions and visual examples are really concise, so you get a bird's-eye view of each color—enough to have a meaningful conversation about what they could mean for your brand.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2UNVlpe

Favorite Quote: "Red is radical. It is extreme, representing passion, energy, fire, violence, and anger. It is vibrant and creates contrast, demanding the viewer's attention." —Sean Adams, p. 83

Quiet: The Power of Introverts in a World That Can't Stop Talking

Whether you're an introvert trying to learn more about yourself or an extravert trying to figure out how the heck to decode the introverts in your life, Quiet by Susan Cain is a great primer on the subject. The book is extremely well-researched and full of anecdotes that will change your thinking on what introversion means in the 21st century.

Purchase Link: https://amzn.to/2D6iVno

Favorite Quote: "In mass, [children] terrify me." —Dr. Seuss, p. 86
